High Level Privly Introduction

This presentation outlines the basic justification and system concepts behind Privly

Updated March 22nd, 2013

(landing page)

Facebook, Gmail, Twitter, Reddit, Baidu, Match, MySpace, OkCupid, Google+, etc.

Modern web companies provide amazing platforms for staying connected with friends and loved ones, but this convenience comes at a cost.

  • Sites own what you post
  • Google reveals Gmail hacking, says likely from China
  • Facebook applications accidentally leak access to third parties

Given time and the unavoidable threats of account compromise, changing design, and human error, a private conversation will inevitably not be private. This poses a challenge: how do we bring a consistent privacy model to the web when we have no control over the communities our friends and loved ones use?

Privly

  1. Grants users their privacy
  2. Adds capabilities, while loosing no existing capabilities
  3. Doesn't challenge the commercial interest of the rich and powerful

Privly sidesteps this issue by allowing users to build on top of any website in a way that: 1. Grants users their privacy 2. Adds capabilities, while loosing no existing capabilities and most importantly, 3. Doesn't challenge the commercial interest of the rich and powerful Let me explain how Privly achieves these goals, but first, I need to explain how Privly works.

Untrusted Websites: Facebook, Gmail, Twitter, Reddit, Baidu, Match, MySpace, OkCupid, Google+, etc.

Trusted Applications: PlainPost, ZeroBin, PGP, etc

Privly adds privacy to websites by integrating trusted applications into untrusted websites.

  • A browser extension: Chrome, Firefox
  • A Host Page: Facebook, Gmail, Twitter, Reddit, Baidu, Match, MySpace, OkCupid, Google+, etc.
  • A "Privly Application": PlainPost, ZeroBin, PGP, etc

The components we are working with are: (Pause a long time between each of these) * A browser extension, this changes the way your browser behaves * A Host Page, this could be any website * And a "Privly Application", which I will explain in a moment.

Look at the video. The series of random colors on the tooltip indicate regions of the page controlled by the extension.

So, let's say you are browsing Facebook and you encounter a specially formatted Privly-type link. If you don't have the Privly extension installed, you can click through to view the link.

However, if you do have the browser extension installed, you can view the same content inside the host page, for example Facebook, without needing to click through to the link.

What you are viewing here is a "Privly Application." It lives in its own separate world so the host page cannot see the content contained within. Privly applications are not limited to text content.

Privly is an "application stack" that allows for more usable security applications.

Photos, videos, content signing, IRC, and cryptography can all be embedded in these applications, and viewed anywhere on the web.

ZeroBin Encryption

ZeroBin Decryption

Another Privly Application is ZeroBin. ZeroBins encrypt their content before sending it to a remote server for storage. To read the ZeroBin content you need access to both the host page, and the encrypted content, wherever it is stored. If you are missing one or the other, you can't read the content.

Privly

  1. Grants users their privacy
  2. Adds capabilities, while loosing no existing capabilities
  3. Doesn't challenge the commercial interest of the rich and powerful

So, coming back to the three goals stated at the beginning, how can we: 1. Grant users their privacy 2. Add capabilities, while loosing no existing capabilities and 3. Not challenge the commercial interest of the rich and powerful

Grant users their privacy: Trusted hosts or use cryptography

First, users can host their content wherever they like. If you can trust your storage provider, you gain privacy. Cryptographic applications can support privacy and security even if you don't trust the content server or the host page.

Add capabilities: Privly Application

Developers call these "injectable applications"

Second, any web application can potentially be injected into the context of a host page if it abides by certain design principles. We can expand the competitive marketplace of functionality inside our favorite communities. This is a gain even for users that don't care about privacy.

Doesn't challenge the commercial interest of the rich and powerful: Give them a choice.

Privly's version of the robots.txt are custom HTML attributes.

Third, Privly respects the rights of website operators. Just like Google respects the robots.txt, Privly allows websites to prevent the Privly extension from running. However, by providing more functionality in situ, Privly increases the time-on-site which is more valuable to website operators than access to your content.
